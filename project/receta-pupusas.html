Pupusas with Loroco



Ingredients


  • 2 cups masa harina (corn flour)
  • 1 ½ cups warm water
  • 1 cup refried beans
  • 1 cup cheese (quesillo or mozzarella works well)
  • ½ cup cooked and seasoned pork (chicharrón)
  • ½ teaspoon salt


Instructions:

Make the Dough: In a bowl, combine the masa harina and salt. Gradually add warm water, mixing with your hands until a smooth dough forms. The dough should be soft but not sticky. Cover it with a damp cloth to keep it moist.

Prepare the Curtido: In a separate bowl, mix the shredded cabbage and carrots. Add vinegar, oregano, salt, and red pepper flakes if using. Toss to combine and let it sit for at least 30 minutes to pickle.

Shape the Pupusas: Divide the dough into 8-10 equal balls. Flatten each ball into a disc (about 4-5 inches in diameter) in your palm. Place a spoonful of your chosen filling in the center, then fold the edges over to enclose the filling, pinching it closed. Gently flatten the filled ball into a thick disc. Cook the Pupusas: Heat a griddle or non-stick skillet over medium heat. Cook each pupusa for about 3-4 minutes on each side, or until golden brown and slightly crispy. Serve: Serve the pupusas hot with curtido on the side and, if desired, salsa roja.
